About Me

I'm a motion capture production specialist with over 6 years at Epic Games (3Lateral/Cubic Motion), working at the heart of high-fidelity performance capture for AAA games and feature films. My career has been built around understanding motion data at a granular level, from capture through to solving and animation delivery, with a sharp eye for quality and a deep appreciation for preserving the nuances of a performance.

In my current role as Lead Motion Tracker, I lead a team delivering facial performance capture data for animation production and digital asset creation. I work closely with animation teams to review solve output for accuracy in timing and facial expression fidelity, and I've led investigations into solver quality that directly improved the fidelity of automated animation output across service projects.

Beyond the tracking work, I develop Python tools and scripts that support artists and productions across the pipeline, from data ingestion and validation through to automated processing workflows and render farm monitoring. I take a collaborative, service-oriented approach to keeping the pipeline healthy and artists productive.

My technical toolkit includes Maya, Unreal Engine 5, Python tool development, node-based pipelines, and render farm operations. I've worked across the full capture-to-animation pipeline on titles spanning AAA games, tech demos and animated shorts.
