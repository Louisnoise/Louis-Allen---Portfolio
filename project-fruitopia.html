Fruitopia

Game Design · Construct 2 · University Project

Fruitopia is a design document and prototype created for a university project — a 2D top-down strategy game about the economic development of a town in the 1970s. I acted as Designer and Director, working with 3 other students to develop a prototype for our end-of-year show.

The project involved collaborating with architecture students researching the layout of commuter towns in the 1970s and their effect on economic growth. We focused on Runcorn and developed a prototype where the population are anthropomorphic fruit and vegetables whose prosperity depends on the player's adherence to their needs.

Concept

Set in a hyper-realistic version of Runcorn, the player takes on the role of the master town planner tasked with developing the area into a prosperous Fruitopia by balancing public satisfaction against financial growth. Through strategic placement of housing and amenities, players witness the effects of architectural trends on the community.

Key Features

The game features a grid-based building placement system where each building has a radius of effect influencing surrounding structures. Borough social leaders make requests that the player must weigh against available development funds. Each borough visually changes between positive and negative states based on population happiness, with decaying areas de-saturating and social leaders physically deteriorating.
